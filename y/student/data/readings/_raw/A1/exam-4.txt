PASSAGE_TITLE: People in Big Cities

PASSAGE:
People in big cities often come from different places. {gap:1} People move to cities to get jobs or to find a better life, and many of them come from other countries. *They* often bring their culture, music and food with them. Visitors to London are often surprised to see so many Indian shops and restaurants, but over a million Indians live in the UK. {gap:2}

Many European cities have large numbers of immigrants. There are Portuguese people in Paris and Turkish workers in Frankfurt. *They* are mostly there from the 1960s and 70s. But American cities have a very long tradition of new citizens from foreign countries. For example, San Francisco has more Chinese people than any city outside Asia. {gap:3} The city is famous for its Chinese restaurants. And New York, of course, is well known for its British and Irish citizens. *The city* has an Irish newspaper and its most important holiday is St Patrick's Day — the national day of Ireland. {gap:4} There are many Koreans from the 1980s and 90s in the US, especially in Los Angeles. In fact there is a large area in the center of Los Angeles that is called "Korea town".

   a) they want to find good jobs

   a) newspapers
   b) traditional food
   c) music, food and culture

   ANSWER: c

   a) India is near London
   b) London has many Indian people
   c) British people like Indian food

   b) France
   c) China
   d) the USA
   ANSWER: d

   a) British holiday
   b) Turkish holiday
   c) Irish holiday
   d) Indian holiday
   ANSWER: c
6. 1980s means __________.
   a) from 1980 to 1989
   b) 1980
   c) 1980, 1981, 1982
   d) 1980 and 1990
