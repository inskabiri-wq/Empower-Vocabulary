PASSAGE_TITLE: Elle MacPherson

PASSAGE:
Eleanor Gow was born in March in 1964. She was the eldest of four children from a wealthy family in Sydney, Australia. Her parents divorced when she was fourteen, and her mother later married Neil MacPherson.

An agent for a modeling company discovered her at the age of sixteen while she was on holiday in Aspen, Colorado, U.S.A. She began modeling part-time, but two years later she left the law course at the University of Sydney to work full-time for the Click modeling agency.

At twenty-one, she married Gilles Bensimon, a French photographer and creative manager of the top fashion magazine "Elle." She modeled for the magazine for six years and she became a supermodel. "Time" magazine called her "The Body."

Elle then moved into movies. However, unlike many other models, Elle was a talented actor. Her first movie was "Alice" (1990). She appeared in many more movies, such as "Sirens," "If Lucy Fell," "The Mirror Has Two Faces," and "Batman and Robin." Elle also starred in several television programs and appeared many times in the popular comedy show "Friends."

Yet Elle is not only a model, actor, and media personality — she is also a successful businesswoman. She has her own company called Elle MacPherson, Inc. The company sells "Elle MacPherson Intimates", women's underwear. She also models for her company. There is also underwear for men. Its name is "Elle MacPherson's Men." The company is the biggest of its kind in Australia and a huge multimillion-dollar success.

Elle also began a restaurant called The Fashion Café with supermodels Claudia Schiffer, Naomi Campbell, and Christy Turlington. She produces her own calendar and has her own fitness video called "The Body Workout."

Her personal life is not quite so successful. She and Gilles Bensimon divorced, and since then, she has had several relationships. Her partner is now a Swiss businessman, Arpade Busson, and the couple has a son called Flynn.
